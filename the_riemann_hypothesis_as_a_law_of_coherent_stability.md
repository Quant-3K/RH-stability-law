# The Riemann Hypothesis as a Law of Coherent Stability (Quant‑Trika)

## Core statement (plain English)

In other words, the critical line is where reality balances the creative push of differentiation (time) with the conserving pull of remembrance (space). At this locus of balance, the system’s coherent structure becomes maximally resistant to real‑direction perturbations—**and that is why the non‑trivial zeta zeros are aligned there**.

- **Universe of discourse.** We treat arithmetic not as a pile of separate facts but as a single informational field in which two complementary tendencies operate:

- **Critical line as balance.** The critical line marks the point where incremental moves “across the real direction” cost the least in the currency of intelligibility. It is not static; it is a **poised equilibrium** where creativity remains readable and memory remains editable.

1. **Field construction:** build a coherence–entropy field from analytic data (e.g., the completed zeta ξ), using a normalization that removes trivial asymmetries and reflects structural information rather than scale.

4. **Zero alignment:** when one then probes along the critical line, the points of maximal resonance (“coherence peaks”) occur at the imaginary coordinates of the non‑trivial zeros. This is not a separate miracle; it’s the signature of standing modes in the field at the place of stability.
